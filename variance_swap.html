The Perpetual Variance Swap

Earlier this year, I attended Lionhack, the blockchain hackathon in NYC organized by the Columbia Blockchain Group. At this hackathon, I developed my passion for blockchain after being inspired from the different projects presented by the guest speakers. One of the other teams competing was a group of students from the University of Michigan. Their project was the concept of an automated market maker, AMM, that could hedge against impermanence loss. I told them it sounded like a great idea, and that if they needed help, I would love to join. A little later, they all came to Chicago for another hackathon, and I joined them to work on the perpetual variance swap project. After a little work later over the summer graphing visuals in Matplotlib, we completed the white paper for the for the first logarithmic automated market maker.
